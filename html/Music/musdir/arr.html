A.R. Rahman,is born as A.S. Dileep Kumar on January 6, 1967, is a globally renowned Indian composer, singer, and music producer. Often referred to as the "Mozart of Madras," Rahman has made a significant impact on both Indian and international music scenes, particularly through his work in film scores and soundtracks .He is called as "Isai Puyal" (musical storm) and "Mozart of Madras" by his fans. Some of his most notable works include soundtracks for films such as "Bombay," "Dil Se," "Taal," "Lagaan," "Rang De Basanti," and "Slumdog Millionaire." The latter, a British film directed by Danny Boyle, earned Rahman two Academy Awards (for Best Original Score and Best Original Song), making him the first Indian to win multiple Oscars.
